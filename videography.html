Living on the edge of two, or sometimes even more, borders set by race, mixed race individuals can experience difficulties identifying themselves. Joining the "right" cultural student group that they belong to and being perceived as a person rather than an exotic being are some of the challenges that make them question how to racially identify themselves. However, as the 2010 U.S. Census indicates, more and more people are identifying as multiracial, embracing the experience.
